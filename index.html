About Me

I am Ph.D. student at the Faculty of Informatics, Università della Svizzera italiana (USI) in Lugano, Switzerland. My research focuses on studying and understanding how individuals' mental states and personality manifest through online social interactions and how such knowledge can be used to address various tasks, such as the early identi cation of mental disorders or personality assessment. Furthermore, I am currently studying how to leverage such knowledge to improve current conversational agents in order to achieve more natural and engaging dialogues with humans, by taking into account user profile information and personality.

Formerly, I was a member of the research and development supporting team at Center for Research, Teaching and Transferring on ICT (CIDETIC), Universidad Nacional de Luján (UNLu), Argentina. I hold a licentiate degree (BSc + MSc) in Information Systems from UNLu. During master's thesis I focused on the development of inverted index management strategies for improving real-time search efficiency (e.g., for microblogging services).
